Project PHP: staff management system

This is a Staff management web application for an au pair staying within the Anderson's household that consists of grandparents, a grandchild who is autistic and needs special care, staff members including a cook, and of course, the au pair.

Note: This is an ongoing project

After completing the requirements analysis for the 'Staff Management System' project (read more about it here), it was time to turn the web application into a reality. My team and I had a design report, prototypes, and feedback from both the customer and the teacher to start programming the real application. As part of our 'Project PHP' class, which serves as a practice course for what was taught in a previous class called 'Web Applications in PHP,' I had the opportunity to work with my team again to develop the web application. This project class included weekly meetings where we had a stand-up meeting, updated the JIRA scrum board, helped each other, asked for help from teachers when needed, brainstormed ideas, coded and pushed to GitHub, and had fun with the team. We also conducted meetings with the client periodically to ensure we met their requirements and expectations. Additionally, we implemented various user support methods that we thought would be helpful, particularly for the elderly and those who require special care. The web framework used was Laravel 9, along with Tailwind CSS, and we also used Livewire, a full-stack framework for Laravel that helps build dynamic websites.

The main goal of the application is to make the au pair's job in managing the household easier, be it managing tasks, appointments, staff members, meals, documents, etc. The client wanted a centralized application where she can view everything and be capable of making changes with a click of a button. This app will also make the staff members' work easier to track and ease communication between all people in the house. It also helps the grandparents keep an eye on everything going on around the household. The grandson is capable of viewing who is in the house and at what time in order to schedule his visits. The app makes all these processes more efficient by improving communication, organization, and accessibility of information. The system focuses on ease of use and speed for the elderly couple, au pair, and staff.

I am serving as the Scrum Master for this project. My responsibilities include updating the JIRA platform, ensuring that everyone is on track and has at least one active task at all times, making daily stand-up meeting reports, and sending them to the teachers. I also take the lead in contacting the client and presenting what the team has accomplished. On the technical side, I am responsible for creating the pages to manage invoices and files. This involves setting up the related database tables, programming the mobile view, ensuring that files are secure, and performing CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete) functionalities on them based on the authorization level of the logged-in user. I have also implemented user support through tooltips and a guided tour of the page. Additionally, I helped out a team member by taking over the task of creating the edit and delete functionalities for absences that staff members may declare. Later on, at the client's request, I implemented an "undo" functionality for the users in case someone accidentally deletes a file or invoice.
