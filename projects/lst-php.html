Project PHP: staff management system

This is a Staff management we application for an au pair staying with Anderson's household that consists of grandparents, a grandchild who is autistic and needs special care, staff members including a cook, and of course, the au pair.

Note: This is an ongoing project

After the requirements analysis project 'Staff management system' (read more about that project here), it was time to make the web application a reality. My team and I now had a design report, prototypes, and both customer and teacher feedback to start working on programming the real thing. As part of our 'Project PHP' class which serves as a practice course for what was taught in a previous class called 'Web Applications in PHP', I had the opportunity to work with my team again in making the web application. This project class included weekly classes where we have a stand-up meeting, update the JIRA scrum board, help each other, ask help from teachers when needed, brainstorm ideas, code and push to GitHub, and of course have fun with the team. Meetings with the client were also conducted every now and then in order to make sure we're meeting their requirements and wants. In addition, we implemented various methods of user support that we thought would be helpful for the users especially since some are elderly and people who need special care. The web framework used is Laravel 9 along with Tailwind CSS. We also used Livewire which is a full-stack framework for Laravel that helps build dynamic websites.

The main goal of the application is to make the au pair's jon in managing the househol easier. Be it managing tasks, appointments, staff members, meals, documents, etc. The client wanted a centralized application where she can view everything and be capable of making changes with a click of the button. This app will also make the staff memebers' work easier to track nd ease communication between all people in the house. It also helps the grandparents keep an eye on everything going around the household. The grandson is capable of viewing wh is in the house and at what time in order to schedule his visits. The app makes all these processes more efficient by improving communication, organization, and accessibility of information. The system focuses on ease of use and speed for the elderly couple, au pair, and staff.

I serve as the scrum master for this project. I am responsible for updating the JIRA platform, making sure everyone is on track and has at least one active task at all times, making stand up meeting reports, and sending them to the teachers. I also take the lead in contacting the client and presenting what the team accomplished. As for the technical side, I'm responsible for making the pages to manage invoices and files. This included setting up the related database tables, programming the mobile view, making sure files are secure, performing CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete) functionalities on them based on the authorization level of the logged in user, and implementing user support through tooltips and a guided tour of the page. I also helped out a team member by taking over making the edit and delete functionalities of the absences that staff members may declare. Later on, per the client's request, I implemented an undo functionality for the users in case someone deletes a file or invoice accidentally.
